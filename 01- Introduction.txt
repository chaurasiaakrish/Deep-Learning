Deep learning is branch of Machine Learning inspired by the structure and functioning of human brain. 
It allows computers to learn patterns and make decisions from large amounts of data, 
automatically discovering important features without being explicitly told what to look for.

Why Deep Learning?

Can handle complex, high-dimensional data like images, text, and audio
Learns non-linear patterns that traditional Machine Learning models often struggle with
Performs automatic feature extraction, reducing the need for manual feature engineering
How It Works (Intuitive)

Think of teaching a child to recognize cats:

Traditional approach (ML): You tell the child, “Cats have pointy ears, whiskers, and a tail.”
Deep Learning approach: You show the child lots of cat pictures, and child figure out the important features on their own.
DL achieves this using artificial neural networks, which are layers of interconnected “neurons” that process and transform data step by step.


Deep Learning doesn’t rely on manually defined rules or features.
Instead, it uses neural networks that automatically learn important patterns and features directly from raw data 
(like images, text, or audio), making it different from traditional machine learning 
where features are often hand-crafted.


Note: Deep Learning doesn’t work with images, text, or sound directly, it first converts everything into numbers. 
Neurons can only understand numbers, during training,
the model learns the right weights and bias automatically by adjusting these numbers to reduce errors.
